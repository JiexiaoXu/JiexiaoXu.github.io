## **Motivation:**
Unlike other American universities, majority of students comes to University of Washington without a major. That is, with few exception of direct admit, most of us enrolled in university with pre-major identity, such as pre-science, and will spend at least two quarters striving to get into the major.

In summer of 2023, my sophomore study had almost came to an end, and fortunately, I got accepted by computer science program offered by the Paul G. Allen School, so I would like to share my experience through this post. Hopefully, this would be helpful for the freshman who are feeling lost in major application and ones who are interested to learn more about udub (short for University of Washington). 

>This post is inspired by [AdroitAmateur](https://www.reddit.com/r/udub/comments/f9s27t/how_to_get_in_to_uw_cs_all_my_stats_and_essays/). Many thanks to his/her reddit for letting me realize that getting into cse major at udub is not impossible.

## **Major System** 

There are three types of major at udub:

Open Major 
: can be declared at any time by any student in good academic standing.

Minimun Requirements Major
: have additional admission requirements (i.e you need to complete several courses to declare major, example: Art) 

Capacity Constrained Major
: require additional requirements and selective screening 

Open major and Minimum Requirement major are not necesarrily worse than Capacity Constrained majors. You can possibly find your passion at Oceanography (open major) or Language Studies (min req major). In other word, discovering a few fun courses and declaring relavant major or minor is totally a good way to enrich your experience at udub.

Notably, a couple of capacity constrained majors is notoriously tough to get into -- [CS](https://www.cs.washington.edu/academics/ugrad/admissions/currentuw) and [ACMS](https://acms.washington.edu/program-requirements-and-information), partially because they have fewer seats and tremendous prospective students. To get into your dream majors, you definitely need to spend more time and pay more effort at study than at your high school.

>It's a saddening **fact** that you need to outperform peers with same aspiration and persevere through a one-year-long or sometime two-year-long study. 
{: .prompt-warning }

>It's recommended to have a **long-term plan** that includes the situation in which you are rejected. I was rejected for twice but my academic path didn't change a lot as I plan ahead. Plus, pairing with person with same goal and forming **study groups** would give you better chance for major application, which is a win-win!

### Background
Since I was only looking at the math-related major at first, so my delineation would primiarily focus on the science track -taking courses like MAHT 12x, Amath, and Stats. 

I graduated from a high school at Connecticut (I am an out-of-state student). It provides some basic AP courses such Calc AB and Physics I. Therefore, I could start from MATH 125 (Integral Calc). it also offers AP Computer Science that sparked my interest at computer science. Therefore, I did have a headstart when I come to udub. However, I would recommend people continue to take Calc BC and Physics Mechanic if their high schools provide, in that you could circumvent the tedious computation work in MATH 125. 

>Remember to check out the resources at [MIT_OCW](https://ocw.mit.edu/). Linear Algebra, taught by Professor Gilbert Strang, is a gem for those who wish to take a peek at college level mathematics (even it's still more about computations). 
{: .prompt-tip }

In comparison to other cse students at high school, I didn't heavily participate in competitions like AMC or AIME, obtain a software internship, or code some cool toy project at Github. That means, you can still be a member of the Allen School regardless that you later find your love in computer science. I initially set my target at Statistic program.  

### Freshman Year 2021 - 2022
#### Fall 
CSE 143 with Professor Stuart Reges GPA:3.8
  : Professor Reges designed CSE 14x series and have persisted to use the traditional grading scale instead of ESNU system. It requires students to hand-write the Leetcode easy problem during the exams while recieve assignments grade out of 20 points. Though I have learned most of Java basics beforehand, Professor Reges went over the entire basics in few lectures. His lecture quality is remarkable, as he could code while making concrete explanation during the lectures. One controversial side of his course is the style points. You have to check line by line to ensure that there is no Gotcha for TA to dock your points. At 143X, you can find quite a few peers in cse major. 

MATH 125 with Professor Alexandra Nichifor GPA:4.0
  : I had online course with Alexandra Nichifor. She explained things well, and her assignments are similar to exams, but Math 125 was designed as weed-out course that set heavy workload at daily homework. I was tired at the computations. It has same concepts as AP Calc AB, and introduced some extra integrating method like disc integration.

ENGL 131 with Instructor Kaelah Rose GPA:4.0
  : It's an usual expository writing course. Write one essay per week and submit your portfolio at the end of quarter. The grading as I heard is based on instructors.

**Remark**: This is my first quarter at udub. Major application was far away, so I chose to take class that is versatile for major requirements. Math, Applied Math, Stats, and Computer Science all demand great performance in MATH 12X series. As you spend time review the homeworks and past exams, you will be fine for exams. Somewhat I feel the entry level course become easier each year as I observed from the exam archive. I decide to take three courses and see if I can deal with the college level study. Students in CSE would probably take MATH 126 and CSE 143 this quarter, preparing them for CSE 311 and CSE 351.    

### Winter  
CSE 414 with Professor Ryan Maas GPA:4.0
  : This is similar with in-major course CSE 344. Professor Maas taught some basic SQL syntax and then introduce high level study of data base system including relational algebra and transactions. Without previous knowledge of computer system, I was lost in the hashing function and some deadlock conditions in transactions. However, the grading at exams is lenient, and homework is doable. The final homework is building a hospital scheduling system.     

MATH 126 with Professor Jonah Ostroff GPA:4.0
  : I took the online option of MATH 126 which focus on the vector calculus. Professor Ostroff went easy on the exams. Homework workload is less than MATH 125's. Therefore, I personally deem that MATH 126 is more of a breeze than MATH 125.
